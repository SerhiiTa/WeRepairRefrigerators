## 1. Purpose and Scope

### 1.1 Why This Policy Exists

WeRepairRefrigerators operates an AI-assisted technician community ("Community") as part of its marketplace platform. This Community is designed to help independent appliance repair Technicians share knowledge, discuss repair challenges, access AI-assisted diagnostics, and support one another professionally.

The Community is a professional space. This Acceptable Use Policy ("Policy") exists to:

- Maintain a respectful, accurate, and trustworthy environment for all participants
- Protect Customers and Technicians from harmful, fraudulent, or misleading content
- Ensure the Community remains a safe resource for technical knowledge sharing
- Define the rules under which participation is permitted and the consequences of violations

### 1.2 Who This Policy Applies To

This Policy applies to all users who access Community features on the Platform, including:

- Registered Technicians participating in forums, discussion boards, AI chat, or peer messaging
- Customers who access publicly visible Community content
- Platform staff and moderators (who are additionally bound by internal moderation guidelines)

### 1.3 Relationship to Other Agreements

This Policy supplements and is incorporated into the Platform's Terms of Service and, for Technicians, the Independent Technician Agreement. In the event of a conflict, the Terms of Service controls.

## 2. Professional Conduct Expectations

### 2.1 Core Standards

All Community participants are expected to engage in a manner consistent with professional trade standards. This means:

- **Be accurate:** Share only information you genuinely believe to be correct. Clearly label uncertainty, speculation, or unverified information.
- **Be respectful:** Disagree with ideas, not people. Critique techniques constructively; do not personally attack other participants.
- **Be honest:** Do not misrepresent your experience, credentials, or qualifications in Community discussions.
- **Be relevant:** Keep posts and discussions focused on appliance repair, platform-related topics, or subjects directly relevant to the Technician community.
- **Be professional:** Write as you would in a professional trade forum. Avoid language or content that would be inappropriate in a workplace setting.

### 2.2 Constructive Criticism

Feedback and disagreement are welcome and valuable. When critiquing another Technician's approach:

- Focus on the technical or procedural issue, not the person
- Provide reasoning or alternative approaches rather than dismissive comments
- Acknowledge that different methods may be appropriate in different situations

### 2.3 No Solicitation in Community Spaces

Community features are for knowledge sharing, not advertising. You may not use Community spaces to:

- Solicit business from other Technicians or their Customers
- Promote your own business, website, or services beyond your Platform profile
- Advertise third-party products, tools, or services without explicit Platform permission
- Recruit Technicians away from the Platform

## 4. Spam Prohibition

### 4.1 What Constitutes Spam

The following activities are prohibited as spam:

- Posting the same or substantially similar content across multiple threads or discussions
- Flooding threads with low-quality, off-topic, or repetitive replies
- Mass or unsolicited private messaging of other Community members
- Automated posting using bots, scripts, or other non-human tools
- Posting affiliate links, referral codes, or commercial URLs without Platform authorization
- Creating posts whose primary purpose is to drive traffic to an external website

### 4.2 Commercial Content

Limited mention of tools, parts suppliers, or services is permitted when genuinely relevant to a technical discussion. However, posts that read primarily as advertisements — regardless of how they are framed — are subject to removal.

### 4.3 Cross-Posting

Posting the same question or content to multiple Community threads simultaneously is discouraged. If your question has already been answered elsewhere, link to the existing discussion rather than duplicating it.

## 5. Fraudulent Technician Prohibition

### 5.1 Misrepresentation of Credentials

You may not represent yourself in Community discussions as holding licenses, certifications, or experience that you do not possess. This includes:

- Claiming to hold trade licenses you do not have
- Claiming EPA 608 certification, HVAC licensing, or other trade credentials without holding them
- Representing yourself as having years of experience significantly beyond your actual background
- Using community credibility to imply expertise you do not hold for the purpose of gaining jobs or influence

### 5.2 Impersonation

You may not impersonate any other Technician, Platform employee, moderator, brand, or public figure in Community spaces.

### 5.3 Fake Accounts and Sockpuppeting

Creating multiple Community accounts to artificially amplify your own posts, votes, or reputation, or to evade a prior suspension, is strictly prohibited.

### 5.4 Job Poaching and Client Diversion

You may not use Community features to identify and approach Customers of other Technicians for the purpose of diverting their business to yourself.

## 6. Dangerous Repair Advice Prohibition

### 6.1 Safety Is Non-Negotiable

Appliance repair involves electrical systems, refrigerants, gas lines, and other hazards that can cause serious injury, death, or property damage if handled incorrectly. All technical advice shared in the Community must prioritize safety.

### 6.2 Prohibited Advice Categories

The following types of content are prohibited regardless of intent:

- **Bypassing safety systems:** Advice on disabling, bypassing, or permanently defeating safety cutoffs, thermal limiters, pressure relief valves, ground fault protection, or other safety devices
- **Unlicensed work on regulated systems:** Instructions for performing work that legally requires a license (e.g., refrigerant handling, gas line work) without disclosing the licensing requirement
- **Dangerous electrical practices:** Advice that violates NEC standards or creates shock, fire, or electrocution hazards
- **Unapproved modifications:** Instructions for modifications not sanctioned by the appliance manufacturer that could void warranties, compromise safety certifications, or create liability
- **DIY advice to unqualified end-users:** Directing Customers to perform complex or hazardous repairs themselves without appropriate safety warnings

### 6.3 Duty to Warn

When sharing technical advice that involves any potential hazard, you must include appropriate safety warnings. If a repair requires professional licensing or specialized safety equipment, say so explicitly.

### 6.4 Platform Moderation of Dangerous Content

The Platform reserves the right to remove any content it determines — in its sole discretion — poses a safety risk, even if the poster believes the advice to be correct. Safety removals are not an endorsement or rejection of any technical position.

## 7. Illegal Content Prohibition

### 7.1 Prohibited Content Categories

The following content is strictly prohibited in all Community spaces:

- Content that violates any applicable federal, state, or local law
- Instructions for, or facilitation of, theft of services or equipment
- Content promoting insurance fraud, warranty fraud, or consumer deception
- Content that infringes any third party's intellectual property rights (see Section 8)
- Content containing or linking to malware, phishing schemes, or harmful code
- Threats of violence against any person or property
- Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or any sexualized content involving minors — this will result in immediate account termination and reporting to law enforcement
- Content that facilitates unauthorized access to computer systems or networks

### 7.2 Regulatory Compliance Content

Advice that encourages or facilitates violation of environmental regulations (e.g., improper refrigerant venting in violation of EPA Section 608) or other regulatory requirements is prohibited.

### 7.3 Reporting Illegal Content

If you encounter content you believe is illegal, report it immediately through the Platform's reporting tool. For content involving imminent danger or CSAM, contact law enforcement directly in addition to reporting to the Platform.

## 8. Copyright and Manual Upload Restrictions

### 8.1 Respect for Intellectual Property

The Community may not be used to distribute copyrighted content without authorization. This includes:

- **Service manuals and technical documentation:** Do not upload, post, or share full copies of manufacturer service manuals, technical bulletins, or diagnostic guides that are copyrighted. Sharing publicly available or openly licensed documentation is permitted.
- **Wiring diagrams and schematics:** Do not post full proprietary diagrams copied from paid databases or manufacturer-restricted materials.
- **Software and firmware:** Do not share proprietary diagnostic software, firmware files, or license keys.
- **Third-party content:** Do not post articles, videos, or other content from external sources without appropriate attribution and a link to the original source.

### 8.2 Fair Use Guidance

Brief quotations from technical documents for the purpose of discussion, commentary, or question-asking are generally permissible under fair use principles. Posting full documents or large excerpts is not. When in doubt, link — don't copy.

## 9. AI-Generated Content Moderation

### 9.1 AI Features in the Community

The Platform's AI-assisted community tools generate responses, suggestions, and knowledge content using artificial intelligence. These tools are designed to assist Technicians — not to replace professional judgment.

### 9.2 AI Content Moderation Standards

AI-generated content on the Platform is subject to automated and human review to:

- Filter responses that contain dangerous repair advice (see Section 6)
- Detect and prevent generation of prohibited content categories
- Flag responses that may be factually incorrect or outdated for human review
- Remove AI outputs that violate this Policy

### 9.3 AI Content Is Not Verified

AI-generated responses in the Community are not reviewed by licensed professionals before publication. The Platform does not warrant the accuracy, safety, or completeness of AI-generated content.

**You must apply your own professional judgment before acting on any AI-generated repair suggestion.**

### 9.4 Reporting Problematic AI Responses

If you receive an AI-generated response that you believe is dangerous, incorrect, or inappropriate, use the thumbs-down / report button on the AI response to flag it for review. Your feedback improves the system.

### 9.5 No AI Jailbreaking

You may not attempt to manipulate, circumvent, or exploit AI systems on the Platform to generate content that would otherwise be prohibited under this Policy. This includes prompt injection, roleplay framing designed to bypass content filters, or any technique intended to cause AI systems to produce harmful output.

## 11. Reputation Abuse Prevention

### 11.1 What Is Reputation Abuse

The Platform's community reputation system (including upvotes, helpfulness ratings, featured answers, and Technician credibility scores) is designed to surface the most useful and trustworthy content. Reputation abuse undermines this system and harms the community.

Reputation abuse includes:

- Using secondary or third-party accounts to upvote your own content
- Coordinating with other users to mutually upvote each other's content artificially
- Downvoting or reporting competitor Technicians' content in bad faith to suppress their visibility
- Brigading — organizing groups of users to collectively vote on content in a coordinated way
- Exploiting algorithmic quirks to artificially inflate content ranking

### 11.2 Consequences

Confirmed reputation abuse may result in:

- Removal of artificially inflated votes or scores
- Temporary or permanent account suspension
- Suppression of content from the affected account in community rankings

### 11.3 Detection

The Platform uses automated systems and manual review to detect patterns consistent with reputation abuse. Detection methods are not publicly disclosed to prevent gaming.

## 14. Moderator and Admin Rights

### 14.1 Moderator Authority

Platform moderators and administrators ("Moderators") are authorized to:

- Remove, edit, or lock content that violates this Policy or the Terms of Service
- Issue warnings to users who violate Community rules
- Temporarily restrict or suspend Community posting privileges
- Escalate accounts for permanent termination review
- Pin, feature, or remove Community posts in the interest of content quality
- Close or merge duplicate threads
- Request additional information from users under investigation for violations

### 14.2 Moderator Discretion

Moderation decisions involve judgment calls. Moderators apply this Policy in good faith but may not always reach the same conclusion in similar cases. The Platform does not guarantee consistent enforcement outcomes in every situation.

### 14.3 Moderator Conduct

Platform Moderators are expected to:

- Apply this Policy consistently and without personal bias
- Not use moderation authority to settle personal disputes or favor specific Technicians
- Recuse themselves from moderation decisions in which they have a personal interest
- Maintain confidentiality regarding reported users and ongoing investigations

### 14.4 No Moderator Liability

Moderators acting in good faith in accordance with this Policy are not personally liable for moderation decisions. The Platform assumes responsibility for moderation conducted by its designated staff.
